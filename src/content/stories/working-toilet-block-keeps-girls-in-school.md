For years, the girls at a Zilla Parishad school in the district had no usable
toilet. Older students, in particular, simply stopped coming during their
periods — and many never came back at all.

This year, MayWe partnered with the school to build a toilet block designed
around the girls' privacy and safety, with a handwash station and a water filter
alongside it. We paired the build with menstrual-health sessions run by a local
teacher trained as a Hygiene Ambassador.

The headmaster's words said it best: *"The girls stopped leaving."* Attendance in
the senior classes is steadier than it has been in years — proof that
infrastructure, not lack of ambition, was the real barrier.

> A clean, safe toilet is not a luxury. For a teenage girl, it is the difference
> between finishing school and disappearing from the register.

This is one school. There are dozens more on our list.
