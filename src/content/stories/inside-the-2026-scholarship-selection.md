Every scholarship cycle, we receive applications from girls whose marks and
determination far outstrip their families' means. Choosing is hard — so we keep
the process clear and fair.

**Eligibility first.** Applicants must be girls aged 14–21, studying in India,
with at least 60% in their last exam and a family income under ₹2 lakh a year,
with priority for tribal, SC/OBC and rural students.

**Merit and need together.** A review panel scores academic record, the
applicant's own essay, and financial need. Shortlisted girls are contacted for a
short conversation.

**Privacy by design.** Income certificates, caste certificates and other
sensitive documents are collected through a secure form, accessible only to our
review team, and **deleted after the cycle closes**. We never publish a child's
identifying documents.

Transparency is not a nice-to-have for an NGO asking people to trust it with
both money and personal data. It is the whole foundation.
