Lakshmi grew up in a tribal hamlet where no girl had ever gone to college. She
scored among the highest in her district board exams — and was about to be married
off, because her family could not see how higher education would ever be paid for.

A MayWe scholarship changed the maths. It covered her tuition, books and travel,
and a volunteer mentor — an engineer in the city — helped her navigate admissions
and her first nervous months on campus.

Today Lakshmi is in her second year of an engineering degree. On her visits home,
she mentors two younger girls preparing for their board exams.

> "I am not the exception," she says. "I am just the first. There are many more
> behind me who only need someone to believe in them."

Every scholarship is a bet on a girl like Lakshmi — and a chance for her to widen
the road for the girls who follow.
