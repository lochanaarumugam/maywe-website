Clean water and a place to wash your hands sound almost too simple to matter. The
results say otherwise.

Six months after MayWe installed handwash stations and a drinking-water filter at
a rural school, teachers report a clear drop in absences caused by stomach illness
and infections. Children who used to miss days at a stretch are in class more
consistently.

The hardware is only half of it. Hygiene Ambassadors — local teachers and women
trained by MayWe — run short, regular sessions so handwashing becomes habit, not
novelty.

Healthier children attend more, learn more, and stay in school longer. That chain
starts with a tap and a bar of soap.
